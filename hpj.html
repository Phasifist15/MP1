High Pressure Jetting Services

The retubing of boilers and heat exchangers is a vital maintenance procedure in various industries, essential for ensuring the longevity and efficiency of these critical systems. Over time, the tubes within these heat exchange units can deteriorate due to factors like corrosion, scaling, and wear, leading to reduced performance and potential safety risks.

The retubing process begins with a comprehensive assessment and inspection of the existing tubes. Visual inspections, non-destructive testing, and the analysis of tube samples help identify the extent of corrosion or damage. Tubes that are found to be damaged or past their service life are carefully removed.

Once the tubes are removed, the interior of the boiler or heat exchanger is thoroughly cleaned to eliminate any accumulated deposits, scale, or residue. This step is crucial to ensure optimal heat transfer efficiency when the new tubes are installed.

New tubes, often made from materials with superior corrosion resistance and durability, are then installed. Precision is essential to ensure proper sealing and alignment. The tubes are typically welded or mechanically attached to tube sheets or headers, depending on the system's design.

Comprehensive testing and quality control measures, including pressure and leak testing, are carried out to guarantee the integrity of the newly installed tubes and the overall system. This includes hydrostatic testing to verify the strength and sealing of the connections.

The final step involves recommissioning the boiler or heat exchanger, ensuring it is in good working condition and can resume its role in heating or cooling processes. The retubing process is a critical aspect of maintaining the efficiency and safety of these systems, extending their lifespan and ensuring reliable operation in various industrial applications.
